What is the difference between a Databricks Certification and a Databricks Accreditation?". Databricks offers two types of tests you can take to validate your skills and earn digital badges shareable with social networks: Certifications and Accreditations. Below is the difference between the two:

  • Certification: A certification is a proctored exam completed through our online examination portal, Webassessor. These exams have stricter rules and requirements to complete, have an associated cost, and a retake policy (you must wait 14 days between unsuccessful exam attempts).
  • Accreditation: Accreditations are free, non-proctored quizzes completed through the Databricks Academy. These have fewer rules and can be attempted as many times as needed (they do not have a re-take policy).

Databricks utilizes a third-party vendor to proctor certification exams - exams are proctored to ensure that testing is fair and consistent across all examinees. For this, Databricks uses Kryterion Webassessor, also referred to as Webassessor, a common vendor for exam proctoring in the technology training industry. Below we include key information about Webassessor you need to know as a Databricks certification exam candidate.

If you haven't already done so, you'll need to create a Webassessor Test Taker account for Databricks. Even if you've created a Webassessor account for another company that uses Webassessor as a proctoring service, you'll still need to create one for Databricks.

Keep in mind that whatever email address you use for your Webassessor account will be linked to your certifications and badges, so be cautious of using a company email for this account in case you have plans of changing employers in the future.

Databricks took an approach where they collaborated with other organizations, ran workshops, and identified twelve key components of Al systems along with the risks associated with each one. They compiled these findings into a resource called the Data and Al Security Framework, or DAS App.

There are two key security tools to focus on. First, Unity Catalog plays a major role in governance and control, especially as new laws require organizations to track what data is used for model training, where models are deployed, and which customers are using them. Unity Catalog streamlines this by centrally managing and securing data and Al assets, controlling access, and tracking lineage for every step, whether the asset is a model or a vector index.

Second, Mosaic Al helps with production, offering features like safety filters and Llama Guard to secure inputs and outputs during model deployment and inference. Evaluation tools such as MLflow tracking also support automated performance monitoring and assessment, making it easier to maintain and prove model security over time.

One critical security component we must consider is Llama Guard. This is a safeguard model designed to significantly enhance the safety of human-Al conversations.
