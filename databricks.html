--- layout: default title: DataBricks --- certification What is the difference between a Databricks Certification and a Databricks Accreditation?". Databricks offers two types of tests you can take to validate your skills and earn digital badges shareable with social networks: Certifications and Accreditations. Below is the difference between the two: • Certification: A certification is a proctored exam completed through our online examination portal, Webassessor. These exams have stricter rules and requirements to complete, have an associated cost, and a retake policy (you must wait 14 days between unsuccessful exam attempts). • Accreditation: Accreditations are free, non-proctored quizzes completed through the Databricks Academy. These have fewer rules and can be attempted as many times as needed (they do not have a re-take policy). Databricks Exam Vendor Databricks utilizes a third-party vendor to proctor certification exams - exams are proctored to ensure that testing is fair and consistent across all examinees. For this, Databricks uses Kryterion Webassessor, also referred to as Webassessor, a common vendor for exam proctoring in the technology training industry. Below we include key information about Webassessor you need to know as a Databricks certification exam candidate. Webassessor Test Taker Account If you haven't already done so, you'll need to create a Webassessor Test Taker account for Databricks. Even if you've created a Webassessor account for another company that uses Webassessor as a proctoring service, you'll still need to create one for Databricks. Keep in mind that whatever email address you use for your Webassessor account will be linked to your certifications and badges, so be cautious of using a company email for this account in case you have plans of changing employers in the future.
